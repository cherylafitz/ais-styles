Guidance for Data Protection and Privacy

Background

Generally, the goals of data protection and privacy laws around the world are to ensure that:

  1. Holders of protected information store and share it in secure, access-controlled environments. This information includes an individual's:
    1. personal data,
    2. personally-identifiable information ("PII"), and
    3. health-related information;
  2. Holders of protected information use it only with the individual's explicit consent, which might allow for uses such as:
    1. evaluating an exchange program application, or
    2. sharing a participant's grade transcript with a host institution.
    It might not allow for uses such as:
    1. contacting the participant for a donation, or
    2. sharing health-related information with a school; and
  3. The individual has a right to access and have control over that data, such as the "right to be forgotten."

Organizations such as American Councils and its partners must follow these laws and regulations.

Personal data and personally-identifiable information is any information that relates to an identified or identifiable individual. This includes when you combine different pieces of information that, together, can identify a particular person, such as home address, age, and gender.

Examples of personal data include:

  • name
  • home address
  • email address, such as name.surname@company.com
  • AIS or other identification number
  • location data, such as the location data function on a mobile phone
  • Internet Protocol (IP) address
  • the manufacturer's advertising identifier of your phone
  • data held by a hospital or doctor, which could be a symbol that uniquely identifies a person

How You Can Protect Data and Ensure Privacy when Using AIS

General Practices

  • Do not share your AIS login(s) with anyone else. This includes not logging into AIS for someone else to use.
  • Do not login to AIS while connected to an unsecured Wi-Fi or other network. You can resolve this by first connecting to a virtual private network (VPN).
  • Always logout of AIS at the end of your workday.

When Accessing or Displaying Data On-screen

  • Keep your AIS web browser window hidden from view when others around you do not have authorization to see that data.
  • Always logout of AIS when you walk away from your desk and when you have finished with your session.

When You Download or Print Excel Spreadsheets, Documents Copies, Reports, or Other Data

  • Keep the document hidden from view when others around you do not have authorization to see that data.
  • Do not leave the document lying visible on your desk when you walk away.
  • Store the document in a secure, limited-access manner, such as a network file space with restricted access or a locked drawer.
  • Destroy the document when you have finished with it. With AIS, you can always download another copy, when needed.
